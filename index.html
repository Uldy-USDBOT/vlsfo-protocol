7. Compatibility Crisis: Managing Complex Blending Risks

Perhaps the most severe operational risk associated with modern VLSFO is the incompatibility between distinct fuel batches. When two seemingly stable fuels are mixed in a vessel's storage tank, their chemical structures can clash, leading to rapid, catastrophic sludge formation. We strongly advocate for absolute segregation policies onboard.

Our research provides step-by-step technical instructions for performing onboard compatibility tests (Spot Tests). Proper management of tank residues and methodical fuel-switching procedures are non-negotiable elements for ensuring continuous, safe vessel operation.

10. VLSFO Stability & Compatibility: Navigating Operational Challenges

While VLSFO is the primary solution for IMO 2020 compliance, it introduced a new era of maritime engineering challenges. Unlike traditional HSFO, VLSFO blends are highly sensitive to Stability and Compatibility.

  • Wax Formation: Due to higher Wax Appearance Temperature (WAT), maintaining precise heating temperatures is critical to prevent filter clogging.
  • Catfines Management: Increased levels of Aluminum and Silicon (Catfines) require advanced centrifugal purification to protect engine liners.
  • The Compatibility Rule: Mixing VLSFO from different suppliers can trigger sludge precipitation. Our advice: Never mix fuels in the same storage tank without a lab-verified compatibility test.
