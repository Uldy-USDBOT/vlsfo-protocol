1. What is VLSFO? Very Low Sulphur Fuel Oil Explained

VLSFO (Very Low Sulphur Fuel Oil) is a marine bunker fuel with a maximum sulphur content of 0.50% m/m, mandated by the IMO 2020 regulation. It replaced High Sulphur Fuel Oil (HSFO) as the standard bunker fuel for global shipping. Pricing is driven by Brent crude benchmarks, refinery margins, and regional demand at major hubs including Singapore and Rotterdam.

3. VLSFO vs HSFO: IMO 2020 Compliance and Sulphur Content

Before IMO 2020, vessels predominantly burned High Sulphur Fuel Oil at 3.5% sulphur. The regulation mandated a switch to 0.50% sulphur-compliant fuel. The key differences between these two grades lie in sulphur content, pricing, and blending stability — VLSFO carries a cost premium but ensures full regulatory compliance.

Many owners also evaluate Low Sulphur Marine Gas Oil (LSMGO) as an alternative. VLSFO is typically cheaper than MGO but requires heating and more careful compatibility management. Understanding the technical and compliance distinctions is essential for sound procurement decisions.

4. VLSFO Technical Specs: Density, Viscosity and ISO 8217

VLSFO must meet ISO 8217 specifications. Key parameters include density (typically 920–970 kg/m³ at 15°C), viscosity (20–180 cSt at 50°C), and CCAI ratings that affect ignition quality. These properties directly influence fuel injection performance and combustion efficiency.

Poor blending stability can cause sludge formation and filter blockages. Engineers are advised to verify compatibility, cat fines content, and pour point before bunkering. Our technical data resources help crews and superintendents manage onboard fuel treatment procedures.
