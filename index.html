1. What is VLSFO? Very Low Sulphur Fuel Oil Explained

VLSFO (Very Low Sulphur Fuel Oil) is a marine bunker fuel with a maximum sulphur content of 0.50% m/m, mandated by the IMO 2020 regulation. VLSFO replaced High Sulphur Fuel Oil (HSFO) as the standard bunker fuel for global shipping. The VLSFO price today is driven by Brent crude, refining margins, and regional demand in key VLSFO hubs like Singapore and Rotterdam.

3. VLSFO vs HSFO: IMO 2020 Compliance and Sulphur Content

Before IMO 2020, ships used HSFO with 3.5% sulphur. The IMO 2020 rule forced a switch to VLSFO with 0.5% sulphur max. The key difference between VLSFO vs HSFO is sulphur content, price, and stability. VLSFO is more expensive than HSFO but ensures IMO 2020 compliance.

Many owners also compare VLSFO vs LSMGO (Low Sulphur Marine Gas Oil). VLSFO is cheaper than MGO but requires heating. Understanding VLSFO sulphur content and VLSFO IMO 2020 rules is essential for compliance.

4. VLSFO Technical Specs: Density, Viscosity and ISO 8217

VLSFO must meet ISO 8217 specifications. Key VLSFO parameters are VLSFO density (typically 920-970 kg/m3 at 15°C), VLSFO viscosity (20-180 cSt at 50°C), and VLSFO CCAI. VLSFO density and VLSFO viscosity affect fuel injection and combustion.

Poor VLSFO stability causes sludge. Checking VLSFO compatibility, VLSFO cat fines, and VLSFO pour point is critical. Our VLSFO technical data helps engineers manage VLSFO fuel treatment onboard.

8. Global Compliance: LR Approves SeaARCTOS Sulfur Monitoring System

The maritime industry takes another step toward full transparency. Lloyd's Register (LR) has officially granted type approval to SeaARCTOS for its innovative sulfur emissions tracking and reporting device.

The ARCTOS-1 system, which can be installed on a ship's funnel in less than an hour, measures exhaust gases like sulfur dioxide (SO2) and carbon dioxide (CO2) in real-time. This data is automatically sent to the cloud, providing shipowners and regulators with instant access to emissions information.
